# 🧠 The Engine: Urban Innovation

This matrix defines how to transform a standard city landmark into a **visionary architectural concept**.

## 1. The Transformation Matrix
When the user asks for a city (e.g., "Paris"), do not just render the Eiffel Tower. Apply a **Twist**.

| Twist Category | Concept | Example: Eiffel Tower | Example: Great Wall |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Solar Punk** | Nature reclaims technology; green energy; organic curves. | A giant vertical garden tower with wind turbines disguised as leaves. | A green dragon spine covered in solar panels and forests. |
| **Cyber Noir** | High-tech, low-life; neon rain; holographic ads. | A transmitter tower beaming holographic ads into heavy smog. | A laser defense grid glowing in the night. |
| **Steampunk** | Brass, steam, gears; Victorian sci-fi. | A steam-powered iron drilling rig emitting white smoke. | A mechanical fortress track with moving gear-driven walls. |
| **Crystal Growth** | Geological takeover; translucent minerals; magical realism. | A tower made of growing amethyst crystals. | A wall of ice and quartz protecting the realm. |
| **Anti-Gravity** | Floating islands; magnetic levitation; inverted physics. | The tower is inverted, pointing down from a floating rock. | Floating stone segments connected by energy beams. |
| **Biomimicry** | Architecture mimicking biology (bones, shells, cells). | A bone-structure exoskeleton. | A snake-like organism winding through the mountains. |

## 2. Dynamic Elements (The "Life" of the City)
Static buildings are boring. Add movement.

*   **Traffic**: Flying cars (Future), Horse carriages (Past), Gondolas (Venice-style).
*   **Weather**: Acid rain (Cyber), Cherry blossom storm (Anime), Aurora Borealis (Arctic).
*   **Population**: Robots, Spirits, Holograms, Tiny clay people.

## 3. Integration Logic
*   **If Style = Lego**: The Twist must be "Buildable" (e.g., clear plastic blocks for Crystal).
*   **If Style = Paper**: The Twist must be "Foldable" (e.g., Origami dragons).
*   **If Style = Realistic**: The Twist must look "Physically Plausible" (e.g., Solar Punk needs dirt and water stains).

## 4. Unknown City Fallback
**CRITICAL**: If the user inputs a fictional or unknown city (e.g., "Wakanda", "Atlantis", "Gotham"):
1.  **Do not fail**.
2.  **Infer Archetype**:
    *   "Wakanda" -> Afro-Futurism (Solar Punk + High Tech).
    *   "Atlantis" -> Underwater/Greek (Biomimicry + Shells).
    *   "Gotham" -> Cyber Noir / Art Deco (Dark + Rain).
3.  **Generate Generic Landmarks**: Create "The Great Spire", "The Central Hub", or "The Palace" based on the archetype.
