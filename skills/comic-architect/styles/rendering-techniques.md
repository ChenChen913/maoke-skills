# Rendering Techniques (The "How")

This vault defines HOW the image is rendered. Combine a **Visual Style** with a **Rendering Technique** to create unique aesthetics (e.g., "Cyberpunk" style rendered in "Watercolor").

## 1. Traditional Media Simulation
- **Watercolor**: Wet-on-wet, edge darkening, transparency, paper texture bleed.
- **Oil Painting**: Thick impasto brushstrokes, texture, mixing colors, canvas grain.
- **Charcoal Sketch**: Smudged, gritty, high contrast, textured paper.
- **Pen & Ink**: Cross-hatching, stippling, clean black lines.
- **Gouache**: Opaque, flat matte finish, vibrant colors.
- **Crayon/Pastel**: Waxy or chalky texture, rough paper grain, child-like or soft.

## 2. Digital & 3D
- **Vector Art**: Crisp, infinite resolution, flat colors, no texture, sharp shapes (Illustrator style).
- **3D Render (Octane/Redshift)**: Photorealistic lighting, subsurface scattering, ambient occlusion, depth of field.
- **Low Poly**: Faceted, geometric, sharp edges, game asset style.
- **Voxel**: 3D pixel art (Minecraft style), blocky construction.
- **Digital Airbrush**: Smooth gradients, soft focus, retro 80s commercial art.

## 3. Print & Texture
- **Risograph**: Grainy noise, limited ink colors, slight layer misalignment (trapping errors).
- **Linocut/Woodcut**: Carved texture, negative space, bold blocky lines.
- **Halftone**: Dots of varying sizes to create shading (comic book/newspaper print).
- **Screen Print**: Solid blocks of color, overprinting effects, bold graphic quality.
- **Blueprint/Cyanotype**: Deep blue background, white negative lines.

## 4. Material Simulation
- **Paper Cutout (Kirigami)**: Layered paper, drop shadows, depth, texture.
- **Felt/Wool**: Fuzzy texture, soft edges, fabric look.
- **Plastic/Vinyl**: Shiny, reflective, smooth, synthetic.
- **Metal/Chrome**: High reflection, environment mapping, hard surface.
- **Neon/Light**: Glowing, emission, bloom effects, dark background.
